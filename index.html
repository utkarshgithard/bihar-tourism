Bihar — Where History Breathes

From the enlightened path of Buddha to the ancient corridors of Nalanda, discover a land of timeless spiritual and historical depth.

Top Destinations

Bodh Gaya

The site where Prince Siddhartha attained enlightenment and became Gautam Buddha.

UNESCO World Heritage: The Mahabodhi Temple complex is a masterpiece of ancient brick architecture. Visit the sacred Bodhi Tree, a direct descendant of the original tree under which Buddha sat. Experience the diverse international monasteries representing Buddhist cultures worldwide.

Nalanda Ruins

Explore the remnants of the world's most prestigious ancient university.

Ancient Ivy League: Established in the 5th century, Nalanda was a global hub for learning, attracting scholars from China, Korea, and Tibet. It once housed 10,000 students and a massive library known as 'Dharma Gunj' (Mountain of Truth).

Rajgir

Known for its hot springs and the majestic Shanti Stupa atop Ratnagiri hills.

City of Kings: Rajgir was the first capital of the Magadha Empire. Take the aerial ropeway to the Vishwa Shanti Stupa or visit Vulture's Peak, where Buddha delivered many of his most important sermons. Don't miss the hot springs with medicinal properties.

Vaishali

The world's first republic and birthplace of Lord Mahavira.

Cradle of Democracy: Established in the 6th century BC, Vaishali is home to the Ashoka Pillar, topped by a single lion. It is also a sacred site for Jains, as the 24th Tirthankara, Lord Mahavira, was born here. Buddha preached his last sermon here before his Parinirvana.

Madhubani Art

Dive into the vibrant, traditional folk paintings of the Mithila region.

Soul of Mithila: Also known as Mithila painting, this art form is characterized by its eye-catching geometric patterns and vibrant natural dyes. Themes range from folklore and nature to the Ramayana. It has gained worldwide acclaim for its unique storytelling style.

Gourmet Bihar

Experience the authentic taste of Litti Chokha and Sattu delicacies.

Rustic Flavors: Litti Chokha represents the heart of Bihar's culinary heritage. The wheat balls (Litti) are traditionally roasted over cow-dung cakes for a smoky flavor and served with ghee and mashed spicy eggplant (Chokha). Extremely nutritious and a must-try for food lovers.

Bihar Through the Seasons

Winter (Oct - Feb)

Best time to visit. Enjoy temple festivals, the Rajgir Mahotsav, and migratory birds.

Monsoon (Jul - Sep)

Witness Bihar in its lush green glory. Perfect for wildlife safaris at Valmiki Park.

Festival (Nov)

Chhath Puja: The most iconic sun-worshipping festival transforms the state into a spiritual hub.
